Building a unified cross-functional process

We aimed to transform the delivery team’s design operations by building a unified design process. My focus was on creating structured workflows, establishing a cohesive design workflow, with an emphasis on cross-functional alignment. UX will be the center of this process as per scope.

By implementing these process-driven strategies, I enabled the delivery team to enhance efficiency, boost collaboration, and consistently deliver high-quality design outcomes that align with their long-term objectives.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Led the creation of a structured process for establishing a design system and facilitated its migration to a new design tool.

  • Enhanced cross-functional collaboration by designing operational workflows that improved alignment between UX and other departments.

  • Guided the UX team through continuous learning and iterative feedback loops to drive consistent quality in their outputs.

Stream 2 Problem Statement

CHALLENGE

The delivery team faced inefficiencies due to a lack of a unified and standardised design workflows, leading to fragmented processes and inconsistencies in design outputs. There was a need to establish a clear and systematic approach to enhance collaboration and streamline operations.

GOAL

My focus was on creating a unified process with UX at its core, enhancing the delivery team's design operations. We aimed to standardise workflows, improve design quality and consistency, and strengthen cross-functional collaboration across their teams through a cohesive design strategy.

THE APPROACH & RESEARCH PLAN

  • We met with team leads to map out current workflows, detailing time spent on each activity and identifying approval tiers. This deep dive uncovered bottlenecks and friction points, highlighting areas for efficiency improvements.

  • We pinpointed specific areas needing enhancement and clearly defined ownership of each task to streamline responsibilities. This step was critical in driving accountability and improving task execution.

  • Analysed the collected data to map inefficiencies and pain points, creating a clear picture of where improvements were needed in the current processes.

  • We developed a service design blueprint to illustrate intersections between team processes and conducted desk research to benchmark against industry best practices, ensuring our approach was aligned with proven standards.

  • A workshop with team leads helped refine the to-be process, integrating practical insights and aligning new workflows with operational capabilities. This collaborative effort ensured the proposed model was effective and scalable.

  • Compiled findings into reports for stakeholders and established a feedback loop to monitor implementation, allowing for real-time adjustments to the process.

OUTCOMES AND KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • Streamlined workflows led to faster decision-making and improved task execution across teams

  • The unified design process improved cross-functional collaboration, aligning teams with strategic goals.

  • Implementation of a service design blueprint created a scalable framework, setting the foundation for future growth and efficiency.

  • Engaging teams through workshops and feedback sessions fosters buy-in and ensures smoother process adoption.

  • A well-structured feedback loop enables real-time adjustments, ensuring the process remains adaptable and aligned with business needs.
