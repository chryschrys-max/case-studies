UX Design team and its processes

A leading global investment firm aims to optimise its design capabilities. My role focused on maturing the UX team's skills and practices, enhancing design operations, and creating seamless workflows for cross-functional collaboration with UX as the center.

By integrating strategic design initiatives, I contributed to strengthening the team’s impact, fostering innovation, and streamlining processes that support their mission to drive long-term value creation.

RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Defined the scope for better estimates and expectation setting

  • Conducted meetings to assess the current state of the UX team and map it against the levels of UX Team Maturity so I can help them in moving to the next level.

  • Developed comprehensive training programs to upskill the UX team both individually and within delivery teams.

  • Led the creation of a structured process for establishing a design system and facilitated its migration to a new design tool.

  • Enhanced cross-functional collaboration by designing operational workflows that improved alignment between UX and other departments.

  • Guided the UX team through continuous learning and iterative feedback loops to drive consistent quality in their outputs.

Since there was no set scope at the beginning of the engagement, I helped define the scope by dividing it to streams

STREAM 1

Building individual skills fosters innovation and strategic thinking, empowering designers to make data-driven decisions. This focus on personal growth elevates team output and leads to more user-centred solutions.

STREAM 2

Structured processes and a unified design system drive consistency and efficiency. This approach enhances collaboration in cross-functional teams, aligns with their goals, and supports scalable growth and innovation.

Stream 1 Problem Statement

CHALLENGE

The individual UX designers lacked a structured development path, resulting in inconsistencies in skill levels and their ability to make data-driven design decisions. There was a need to enhance their analytical and design capabilities to ensure that each team member could contribute effectively to strategic initiatives.

GOAL

To create a targeted training program that upskills each designer, focusing on improving their research, design, and analytical skills. The aim was to empower them to make informed design decisions, increase their confidence in strategic roles, and elevate the overall quality of UX outputs.

THE APPROACH & RESEARCH PLAN

  • Conducted one-on-one sessions with each designer to evaluate their current skills and identify areas for growth. This phase also included discussions with stakeholders to ensure the training objectives aligned with strategic goals, laying the groundwork for a focused development plan.

  • Analysed the team's existing capabilities to pinpoint strengths and skills gaps, using this insight to craft a customised training framework. By benchmarking against industry best practices, we ensured that the program addressed both individual growth and organisational needs.

  • Personalised growth plans were developed for each designer, targeting specific competencies like user research and data-driven design. We also organised collaborative workshops to promote knowledge-sharing and cultivate a learning-oriented team culture.

  • Hands-on training sessions were conducted to equip designers with advanced design principles and techniques. These workshops provided practical opportunities to apply new skills through real-world projects, reinforcing their learning.

  • We gathered feedback from the team to assess the effectiveness of the training sessions, refining the approach as necessary. Regular adjustments were made to ensure that each designer’s progress was aligned with both their personal goals and expectations.

  • A continuous feedback loop was established to support designers as they applied their new skills in their projects. We also set up KPIs to measure the training program's impact, with ongoing catch-up sessions to address challenges and celebrate achievements.

OUTCOMES AND KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • Individual designers gained stronger skills in user research, data-driven decision-making, and interaction design, significantly boosting their contributions to strategic initiatives.

  • Designers became more confident in their abilities to apply advanced design principles, leading to higher-quality outputs and more effective design solutions.

  • The personalised growth plans ensured that each team member’s development was closely aligned with strategic objectives, resulting in a more cohesive approach to UX design.

  • Investing in individual skill development not only empowers designers but also elevates the overall performance of the team, leading to more impactful design outcomes.

  • Tailored growth plans that address specific needs and strengths of each designer are essential for maximising their potential and aligning their skills with business goals.

  • Regular feedback loops and iterative improvements are crucial to adapting training methods, ensuring that the team’s learning trajectory remains relevant and effective.

  • Aligning individual development with broader organisational objectives enhances the team's ability to contribute strategically, making them valuable assets to the vision.
