Behavioural anchor — Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change)

This study uses the Transtheoretical Model as a lens for understanding readiness to change. The model describes five stages: Precontemplation (not yet thinking about change), Contemplation (aware of the problem, not yet acting), Preparation (planning to act), Action (making the change), and Maintenance (sustaining it).

Participants in this study sit at different stages for different health behaviours. This partly explains why the same concept landed differently across sessions — it is not just about the concept design, it is about whether the concept meets a participant at the right stage. See XP5 in the cross-concept section for the full pattern.

Insight — XP5
The concepts are not landing differently because of design alone. Participants at Stage 1 or 5 do not need any of the three concepts — they are either not yet aware or already sorted. The concepts are most relevant for Stages 2 to 4, and each concept may suit a different stage. C1 and C3 suit Contemplation and Preparation. C2 suits Preparation and Action. This has implications for how and when the concepts are distributed.
→ Distribution timing and entry point matter as much as the concept design. Consider staging the offer by readiness signal, not just by age.
